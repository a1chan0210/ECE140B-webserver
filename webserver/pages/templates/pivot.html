Pivots

Pivots Done

Through our customer market analysis we've done, we've found that parents like daily task feature and how it motivates their children to complete tasks like walking around and taking care of their Pep Pets. We've decided to expand this feature and allow parents to add custom tasks to add things that they want their children to do like doing chores such as cleaning dishes, taking out the trash, etc and the parents will be able to give them custom rewards.

Zoom-In Pivot

The pivot we've done is a Zoom-In pivot. This is because we have found one of our features that our customers like, the daily task feature, and have improved it. By giving the parents the ability to custominze the daily tasks, they can add how they want to raise their children and add ways that they want to teach their children about taking up responsibilities.

Future Pivots

In the futures, we may plan to expand the customer base. The daily task feature can also be useful in an educational setting to reward students for completing homework or doing extra activities such as reading a book, or completing practice assignments. We would like to develop PepPets to be easily integrated to the classroom and potentially be able to link multiple PepPets in a classroom to have the same educational tasks.

Platform Pivot

This is a platform pivot. We are finding a different avenue that might be in need of our product. Rather than a handheld toy that empulates a pet and teaches responsibilities, we can focus on a educational setting and pivot towards an educational aid tool to help motivate students in a classroom setting and add more engagement to their studies.

Considered Pivots

Some pivots have been suggested like adding microtransactions to unlock more collectable items. However, we do not believe that we should do this pivot. PepPets is a tool to teach young children responsibilities. We want this to be less like a game and lean more towards the educational tool. Adding microtransactions adds the risk of children irresponsibly spending their parents' money and distracts them away from the main purpose to learn to be responsible.
